X-ray, or radiography, is a medical imaging technique that uses electromagnetic radiation to create images of the inside of the body. In X-ray imaging, a small amount of ionizing radiation passes through the body, and the resulting shadows are captured on a detector to produce images of bones, organs, and other structures. X-rays are commonly used for diagnosing fractures, infections, and abnormalities in the chest or abdomen. The procedure is quick, non-invasive, and widely employed in medical settings. However, it involves ionizing radiation, and precautions are taken to minimize exposure.

Ultrasound is a medical imaging technique that uses high-frequency sound waves to create real-time images of internal body structures. It is non-invasive, safe, and commonly used to visualize organs, monitor pregnancies, and assess blood flow. The procedure involves a handheld transducer and gel applied to the skin, producing images through sound wave echoes. Ultrasound is valuable for its real-time capabilities and absence of ionizing radiation.

MRI, or Magnetic Resonance Imaging, is a medical imaging technique that uses strong magnetic fields and radio waves to generate detailed images of the internal structures of the body. It is non-invasive and provides high-resolution images of soft tissues, making it valuable for diagnosing a wide range of medical conditions. During an MRI scan, the patient lies inside a large, tube-like machine, and the technique is particularly useful for imaging the brain, joints, organs, and soft tissues. Unlike X-rays or CT scans, MRI does not use ionizing radiation..
